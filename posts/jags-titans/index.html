The Rise and Collapse of the Jaguars and Titans in 2022

In the 2022-2023 NFL season the Jacksonville Jaguars and the Tennessee Titans had an entwined destiny that seemed impossible to come to fruition at the half way point of the season. The Jaguars were 2-6 at one point in the season while the Titans once sat at 7-3.

Jacksonville after that point would go on an incredible run to end the year while Tennessee suffered major injuries and took a nosedive. This all led to the Jaguars and Titans meeting in the final week of the year to see which team would move onto the playoffs.

Before we talk about the final game however, we need to look at some stats that lead to the teams being in that position in the first place. We can start by looking at the point differential for the Jaguars and Titans losses.

The point differential of the two team’s losses is a valuable stat because it shows that even when the teams were losing, how close they may have been to actually winning those games if a couple things go differently.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggalt)
library(gt)
library(ggbeeswarm)
library(ggrepel)
library(ggtext)
library(waffle)

jags <- read_csv("data/jaguarsseason.csv")
titan <- read_csv("data/titansseason.csv")
nflstats <- read_csv("data/nflstats.csv")

jaguars <- jags%>%
  group_by(Game) %>%
  mutate (
      PointDifferential = (ScoreTm-ScoreOpp)) %>%
  select(Game, PointDifferential, ScoreTm, ScoreOpp)

ggplot() + 
  geom_dumbbell(
    data=jaguars,
    aes(y=reorder(Game, PointDifferential), x=ScoreTm, xend=ScoreOpp),
    
    
    size = 2,
    colour = "grey",
    colour_x = "green",
    colour_xend = "red")+
  theme_minimal()+
  labs( 
    x = "Score",
    y = "Game",
    title="Jacksonville stayed close even in losses", 
    subtitle = "The <span style = 'color:green;'>Jaguars Score</span> is typically close with the  <span style = 'color:red;'>Opponent Score</span> in losses showing how close their losses often were.",  
  caption="Source:Pro Football Reference|By:Sampsun Martenson") +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face= "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size=9),
    axis.text.x = element_text(size=9),
    axis.title.y = element_text(size=9),
    plot.subtitle = element_textbox_simple()
  )

Jacksonville’s losses were often close showing how they were better than their record, especially early on in the season. On the other side the Titans had many games where their wins were close and their losses were often blowouts. This goes into showing how Tennessee’s record may have not been as solid as it seemed.

We can then look at the total total first down differential for the Jaguars and Titans. This was an interesting faze of the games to look at for both of these teams due to how it showed just how both offenses compared in consistency.

Teams can often have less first downs if they are a more explosive team such as the Chiefs but neither Jacksonville or Tennessee’s offense is focused on big plays. Both teams often try to meticulously move down the field through short but consistent gains of yards.

This is where it becomes noticeable just how much Jacksonville dominated this category. The Titans had a total of 277 first downs compared to 352 by the Jaguars. Put this into an entire season and it shows how Tennessee’s offense often couldn’t get anything consistent going while Jacksonville was very consistent while moving the ball.

Finally we can look at the difference in passing offense between the two teams. The Jaguars had a quarterback in Trevor Lawrence who was able to be relied on, especially in the second half of the year, along with other offensive weapons that helped them have a good passing attack.

This was a large component of what allowed the Jaguars to have such a large turnaround in the second half of the season, the passing game found its footing.

On the other side of the coin was the Titans passing offense which for a large portion of the year was quarterbacked by Ryan Tannehill. He wasn’t able to set any records but he was a passable leader to rely on for the passing game.

This changed when Tannehill was injured during the season and Tennessee had to scramble to try to find a competent backup. This is the reason for the passing game taking a nosedive late in the year for the Titans along with being a large reason for the Titans late season collapse.

After everything was said and done the Jaguars and Titans met each other in the last week of the regular season in a mini playoff game where the winner went to the playoffs. The Jaguars ended up using all of these different facets of their game that improved over the course of the season and beat the Titans to move onto the playoffs.

The Titans over the course of the season had many things go wrong for them to have the unfortunate end to the season that they did. The Jaguars had many things start working for them at about the same time everything started falling apart for Tennessee.

Jacksonville has now found a way to play that fits them well through all of this and with some new additions to the team they may be able to keep up the impressive showing they had towards the end of this season for years to come.
