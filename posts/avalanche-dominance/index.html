The Dominance of the 2022 Colorado Avalanche

hockey
colorado
analysis
Author

Sampsun Martenson

Published

March 21, 2023

In 2022 the Colorado Avalanche hockey team had an incredibly dominant playoff run which ended with them lifting the most coveted trophy in the sport, the Stanley Cup. There were many different factors that contributed to the overall domination.

The three main factors being overtime victories, point differential, and how the goal scoring was able to carry the team when the goalkeeping and defense may have let the team down.

To show how great the Avalanche were at winning in overtime let’s compare them to the other playoff teams in 2022.

This stat is a great place to start to show just how shut down and resiliant this Avalanche team was. They went into overtime a total of six times and won all but one of them over the course of their playoff run. This also included two overtime victories in the finals.

The next stat we can look to is point differential to show just how ridiculous this Avalanche team was when scoring goals compared to other playoff teams.

The final piece of this puzzle comes from how dominant the offense was able to be even when they may have been allowing more goals per game than the average playoff team.

Code 
average <- playoffs%>%
  group_by(Team) %>%
  mutate (
    AverageGoalsScored = (G/GP),
    AverageGoalsAllowed = (GA/GP)) %>%
  select(Team, AverageGoalsScored, AverageGoalsAllowed)
Code 
ggplot() +
